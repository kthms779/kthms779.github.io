Deepfakes, 5G among top risks to cybersecurity in 2020: Forcepoint (12/12/19):
Deepfakes are images or videos created by computers or machine learning software which appear real. According to this article, “in politics, the technology is used to discredit candidates and influence voters through inaccurate political messages on social media platforms.” With the already abundance of fake news in the world and the coming 2020 US presidential elections, I shudder to think of what this technology could do to people’s understanding of various circumstances. The technology can be used for many other purposes too- cybercriminals can use it to generate compromising photos or videos of individuals and blackmail them. It’s remarkable to see how AI has developed to be able to achieve this.

What Is The Artificial Intelligence Of Things? When AI Meets IoT (20/12/19):
With the combination of AI (Artificial Intelligence) and IoT (Internet of Things), I think humanity will evolve much more rapidly. Incorporating AI into everyday tasks such as in driving vehicles will increase the efficiency of the device, in my opinion. For instance, taking the vehicle example the article mentioned, we are able to easily collect data in vehicles from sensors, radars and the GPS, which can be uploaded onto the internet, allowing for the AI to monitor vehicle maintenance, driver behaviour, and more. AIoT has already been implemented in Tesla’s autopilot systems.

Artificial intelligence jobs on the rise, along with everything else AI (21/12/19):
This article talks about a recent study in Stanford University called the ‘AI Index’, in which they have tracked the growth of metrics and evaluated the use of AI in the world. The full details are in the article, however the findings on AI jobs particularly has really intrigued me. It states that “looking at Indeed postings between 2015 and October 2019, the share of AI jobs in the US increased five-fold since 2010”. The increased usage of AI in tech-related jobs is evidence of the continuous advancements that have been made, and I believe there will be widespread usage of AI in almost all sectors in the near future. I predict that 2020’s AI Index readings will show even more growth.
