Police launch homicide inquiry after German hospital hack (18/09/20):
This tragic situation in Germany goes to show the harmful side of hacking when done for malicious purposes. The hackers attacked the hospital unintentionally rather than their target which led to the death of a woman whose life could have been saved- they could not transfer her to another hospital in time as it was too far away. Another point to note is that the hacker were able to access the hospital due to a flaw in the Virtual Private Network (VPN) that was developed for them by Citrix. Despite numerous warnings about the vulnerability, no action was taken and so it was exploited, leading to this tragedy. It goes to show the need to be watchful and be 100% secure, especially when the data is so sensitive.

Artificial Intelligence and I Discuss What Happened Before the Big Bang (14/09/20):
The article discusses OpenAI’s GPT-3 language model, an autoregressive language model that uses deep learning to produce human-like text. Seeing this article was a pleasant surprise and it demonstrates how far AI has become. The fact that the GPT-3 language was able to converse with the interviewer about a topic (the Big Bang) that many, including myself, don’t fully understand demonstrates the vast amount of information an AI programme can store and represent. In the future, an AI programme could store much more data than the human mind and that would be truly amazing.

Britain plans hiring spree to harness big data in Covid-19 pandemic recovery (9/09/20):
Britain’s Prime Minister Boris Johnson has called for the training of hundreds of data scientists as part of ‘The National Data’ project to drive an economic recovery after the COVID-19 pandemic through the better and smarter use of data and technology in policy-making. Digital Minister Oliver Dowden notes that if the country shares high-quality data quickly, efficiently and ethically, it will be able to recover much better. This is certainly the case right now when passing the data of the number of infections in a particular location to local authorities is crucial to stop outbreaks.
