Facial Recognition Software Has a Bias, New Government Report Finds (20/12/19):
The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) study found that African Americans and Asians were up to 100 times more likely to be misidentified than Caucasians depending on the individual algorithm, which is interesting. NIST looked at 189 algorithms, so the general conclusion can be assumed to be similar for most algorithms. From what I understand, this is because the facial training data sets skew too heavily to whatever the majority race is where the algorithm is developed, and Asian/African American data sets may not be used sufficiently in some algorithms.
